NewsOlympic Torch Relay Reading route confirmedReading Borough Council has confirmed the route of the Olympic Flame on its way in and out of the town on July 10 and 11. ShareBygetreading00:00, 19 MAR 2012Updated19:25, 7 JUN 2013NewsThe Olympic Torch will parade through Reading on July 10 and 11. Visit the interactive map at: http://l2012.cm/yRglRC ShareGet daily updates directly to your inbox+ SubscribeThank you for subscribing!Could not subscribe, try again laterInvalid Email Reading Borough Council has confirmed the route of the Olympic Flame on its way in and out of the town on July 10 and 11.
Residents can plan their ‘roadside seats’ as torchbearers carry the historic flame into the town, bringing it into the heart of local communities and allowing people to share in the excitement of the Olympic Torch Relay.
The Torch is scheduled to enter the borough from the east at around 6pm on Tuesday, July 10 at Sutton Seeds roundabout, before travelling along London Road , crossing the IDR and being paraded along the riverside at The Oracle.

Each Torchbearer will wear a white and gold uniform which been designed by adidas. <br />   <br /> Marian Livingston, Reading Borough Council’s Lead Councillor for Culture and Sport and Chair of the Reading Cultural Partnership, said: “Today’s announcement of the route the Olympic flame will take across Reading will truly make this real for local residents. It will be so exciting to see the flame pass through so many local communities across our town and I know the people of Reading will give it a marvellous welcome. This really is a once-in-a-lifetime event and today’s announcement of the route means people can start to plan for it.” <br />   <br /> Andy Murrill, Chair of Reading 2012 Partnership, said : “It is wonderful that the torch relay is coming through Reading over two days for everyone to have the chance to experience a part of the London Games. </p> <p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> "Now the exact route is known people can start to plan where they will watch the torch go past with their families and friends. I hope everybody will have an Olympic experience to remember and give the torch relay runners a really warm Reading welcome.” <br />   <br /> Sebastian Coe, Chair of LOCOG added: "Today we bring the Olympic Torch Relay to life. The Flame symbolises the Olympic spirit and its journey around the UK will bring the excitement of the Games to our streets." </p> <p xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"> The event at Madejski Stadium event  will be free.
